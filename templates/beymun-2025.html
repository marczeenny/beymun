Best Delegate Award

This is the highest award in the committee.
Criteria:

  • Consistently drives substantive debate forward.
  • Demonstrates mastery of the Rules of Procedure, using them effectively and correctly.
  • Delivers clear, persuasive, and impactful speeches.
  • Actively contributes to draft resolution creation and leads initiatives.
  • Works cooperatively with a wide range of delegates to build alliances.
  • Plays a major role in writing, amending, or defending resolutions.
  • Engages actively in both moderated and unmoderated caucuses.
  • Handles and resolves crises and challenges diplomatically.
  • Meets the criteria for the Best Position Paper award.

Diplomacy Award

Recognizes delegates who excel in cooperation, negotiation, and bridge-building.
Criteria:

  • Demonstrates respectful and inclusive behavior toward other delegates.
  • Focuses on compromise and solution-driven approaches.
  • Mediates tensions and fosters collaboration.
  • Builds consensus even among conflicting blocs.
  • Maintains diplomatic decorum at all times, even under pressure.
  • Recognized as a peacemaker or coalition-builder.
  • Actively participates in lobbying without dominating discussions.
  • May not be the loudest speaker but significantly contributes to the committee’s productivity.

Best Position Paper Award

Awarded for excellence in pre-conference preparation and content.
Criteria:

  • Demonstrates a deep understanding of the topic, background, and country policy.
  • Incorporates well-researched, properly cited facts, statistics, and relevant events.
  • Proposes feasible and creative resolutions backed by previous frameworks and actions.
  • Follows a clear structure, correct formatting, and adheres to submission guidelines.
  • Contains no AI-generated or plagiarized content.
  • Demonstrates proficient use of relevant, professional, and formal terminology.

Honorable Mention Award

Recognizes standout delegates who impressed the chairs and made a notable impact.
Criteria:

  • Delivers passionate, captivating, and creative speeches.
  • Maintains a memorable presence in the committee through wit, bold stances, or unique perspectives.
  • Demonstrates growth or significant improvement throughout the conference.
  • Makes a significant contribution to a key event, clause, or bloc that warrants recognition.
  • May be a new delegate who demonstrated satisfactory conference performance despite limited experience.

Best Delegation Award

Awarded to the school or university delegation that demonstrates the highest overall excellence across all committees.
Criteria:

  • Earns the highest cumulative number of delegate awards, with greater weight given to higher-tier awards.
  • Maintains a high level of professionalism, teamwork, and diplomacy throughout the conference.
  • Demonstrates strong delegation unity, coordination, and strategic involvement in committees.

Best Chair Award

Recognizes the most outstanding committee chair for exceptional leadership, fairness, and contribution to the conference.
Criteria:

  • Maintains impartiality, professionalism, and control over committee proceedings.
  • Demonstrates an in-depth knowledge of the topic, Rules of Procedure, and diplomacy.
  • Guides debates effectively, ensuring a productive and engaging committee environment.
  • Provides constructive feedback and assistance to delegates to enhance their performance.
  • Facilitates a fair and inclusive discussion where all delegates have the opportunity to speak.
  • Handles crises, procedural matters, and challenges with composure and authority.
  • Plays a key role in making the conference a valuable learning experience for all participants.
